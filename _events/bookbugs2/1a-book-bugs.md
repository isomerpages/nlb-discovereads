<u>Competitive Play Clarifications made by Gamemasters on Day 1</u>

1. In your deck of 20 cards, you can have one of each Guardian and Skorpius. This means you can have a Nostro, a Shenlong, a Skorpius s but you cannot have 2 Nostros.
2. When Nym9 or Yeticus is played on the field, it cannot be discarded, not by any of the Discard or Return to Hand effects. For ‚ÄúDiscard the last two cards on an opponent‚Äôs field‚Äù, you also cannot jump over a Nym9/Yeticus and discard the 1st and 3rd cards.
3. But if Nym9 or Yeticus are both in your Draw pile or Hand and have not been played onto the playing field, then it can be discarded.
4. If ‚ÄúLook at an opponent‚Äôs hand‚Äù card effect is played, only the player who played it gets to look at the cards.
5. If ‚ÄúYou can play an additional card on this turn‚Äù card effect is played, the additional card‚Äôs effect does not count and it only contributes towards game points.
6. Players can opt not to use their card effect.
7. If ‚ÄúAll players to draw an additioal card‚Äù is played, this applies to the person that played the card as well.
8. If ‚ÄúDiscard the top 2 cards from all player‚Äôs Draw piles‚Äù is played, this DOES NOT apply to the person who played the card.
9. If Nullify is played, and the second player plays another Nullify card, nothing happens because the second Nullify card is nullified. Third player continues as per usual.
10. If Nullify is played, and the second player plays Nym9 or Yeticus, they will continue have their card effect and cannot be discarded.
11. Sudden Death only applies to tied players.
12. The game ends when ONE player‚Äôs Draw pile is empty and that player has played their final card. If they end because of an card effect like ‚ÄúDiscard any number of cards in your hand. Draw the same number of new cards from the Draw pile‚Äù, then the game ends and the player cannot play another card.
